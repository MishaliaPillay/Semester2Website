Data-Visualisation

That Asteroid came outta nowhere!

This Page shows the data from the NASA NEOWs (Near Earth Object Web Service)API. This data looks at asteroids and their distance to Earth as well as other planets close to Earth such as Venus and Mercury.

The first visualisation looks at the asteroid data of 3 planets over 102 years. The criteria for asteroids on this graph are that they are of the same magnitude which is 21.87 h and that they are all potentially hazardous. This is interesting because it conveys a narrative that Earth has the most amount of asteroid encounters. The second visualisation looks at the asteroid data of Earth for 7 days. Only a few of these asteroids are hazardous and they vary in size which is shown by the different radii.

This data visualisation focuses on the closest asteroids to Earth between the dates (07 August 2023 -14 August 2023 ). The graph shows asteroids that are and are not potentially hazardous. The potentially hazardous asteroids are red.This graph compares the sizes of the asteroid, shown by varying radii.
